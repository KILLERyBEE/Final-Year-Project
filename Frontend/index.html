The Start Camera option activates the system's webcam and begins real-time gesture detection, allowing users to control their device through hand movements. Once enabled, AirNav starts tracking gestures and converts them into system commands such as scrolling, zooming, and media control. The Stop Camera option safely disables the webcam and pauses gesture recognition, ensuring privacy and giving users full control over when the system is active.

Core Features

AirNav uses computer vision and deep learning to detect hand gestures in real-time, converting natural hand movements into precise system commands. Powered by MediaPipe and custom CNN models, our system supports a wide variety of gesture-driven interactions.

Document Control

Navigate through documents and files using intuitive gestures. Swipe, scroll, zoom, and flip through pages without touching a single key. AirNav's document control module recognises open-palm swipes for page turns and pinch gestures for zooming, enabling seamless reading and editing workflow automation.

Zoom Control

Effortlessly zoom in and out of your screen or applications with a simple pinch gesture. The zoom control module intelligently detects the distance between your thumb and index finger, translating finger spacing into smooth, lag-free zoom transitions across browsers, documents, images, and maps in real-time.

Learn More →

Slide Navigation

Transform your presentations with hands-free slide control. A right-swipe gesture advances to the next slide while a left-swipe goes back. The system supports laser-pointer simulation via hand position tracking, helping speakers deliver impactful presentations without a physical clicker or remote.

Learn More →

Volume Adjustment

Control system audio intuitively with vertical hand gestures. Raising your hand increases volume while lowering it decreases it. AirNav maps the vertical position of your palm to the system audio level in real-time, supporting both smooth gradual changes and quick mute toggles via a fist gesture.

Learn More →

Video Playback Control

AirNav supports media controls including play, pause, fast-forward, and rewind — all through mid-air gestures. A closed fist pauses playback, while pointing sideways seeks forward or backward. The system integrates with system-level media APIs, giving you universal control over any video application or stream.

Learn More →

Scroll Control

The scroll control module tracks the orientation and position of your hand to scroll web pages and documents naturally. Moving your index finger upward scrolls the content up, while pointing it down scrolls down. The system supports velocity-sensitive scrolling for both subtle fine-control and rapid navigation across long pages.
